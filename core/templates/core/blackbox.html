Black box Investigation

The mystery black box is a box that has a funnel in the top and a tube out of the bottom. When you pour water in the top, you can hear water flowing but no water comes out for a while, you often have to input over 1 liter of water until some flows out. The box often outputs more water then was just put in in the last pour. In this investigation the goal was to replicate the behaviors of the mystery black box.

Claim

I believe that the mystery black box has 2 syphons in it that store water and release it when they fill up.

Evidence

We poured a certain amount of water in the top of the black box, and we then measured how much water came out the bottom. For test 3 we poured in 600ml of water, and 1250ml came out. When we poured different amounts of water in the top, we did the same and measured how much water came out of the bottom. On test 3 of our box we poured in 1000ml of water and 450ml came out.

Using the data we collected, we made a hypothesis that there were 2 syphons in the black box. After that we cut a hole in a cardboard box and fastened two buckets to the inside of the box. We used the buckets to make two syphons. Once we finished the entire model, we started to test it and record our results to compare to the original box.

Reasoning 1

A model is something that simply shows the function of a structure or design visually.

Reasoning 2

My evidence supports my claim because the variability in our models output is similar to the variability in the black box. Often we would pour water in the top of our design, and the amount of water that came out of the bottom was very random and unpredictable. The mystery black box had a similar level of randomness but not the exact same.

Reasoning 3

If a claim about a tipping bucket was correct, then the amount of water outputed would always be above a certain number. This is because the bucket would only tip after a certain amount of water was poured. For example, if the bucket flips over after 500ml of water, when you pour in 900ml of water, like in test 2, water would guarantee pour out because its over the threshold.

3D modeling, Animation, and Augmented reality

Intro and background knoledge

Autodesk MAYA is a 3D modeling program that is used to develope virtual 3d models. Augmented Reality is a way to enhance real life with virtual models, its a combanation of Virtual Reality and real life.

During this project we used MAYA to create a 3d model of what we thought was in the black box. I first made a hollow box to contain all of the innards, then I created a funnel to put in the top of the box just like the real black box. After that I modeled a bucket which I then put in the box. Once I finished all of the innards of my box, I started to animate a bucket pouring water into the funnel in the top of my box. I then worked on animating the buckets in the black box filling up. Once I was finally done animating my whole black box in MAYA, I moved to Mergeedu.com to turn my black box into Augmented reality.
